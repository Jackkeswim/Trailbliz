Unique of Mount Korbu

Location: Titiwangsa Range in Peninsular Malaysia

Mount Korbu, standing tall at 2,183 meters (7,162 feet), is the second-highest peak in Peninsular Malaysia, and a favorite among seasoned hikers. Nestled within the Titiwangsa Range in Perak, it is part of the G7 Peaks, a group of the seven highest mountains in Peninsular Malaysia, making it a prestigious target for climbers seeking to conquer these iconic heights.

Mount Korbu, nestled in the heart of Perak, is not only a challenge for hikers but also a mountain with unique geological and ecological significance. As the second-highest peak in Peninsular Malaysia, it forms part of the Titiwangsa Range, a mountain range that acts as the backbone of the peninsula. The land around Mount Korbu is special due to its rich and diverse ecosystems, which play a vital role in maintaining the biodiversity of the region.

The land surrounding Mount Korbu is predominantly covered by tropical rainforests, creating a unique environment with high humidity and abundant rainfall throughout the year. The forests here are teeming with life, from towering trees to rare and endemic plant species, such as mosses, orchids, and ferns.
